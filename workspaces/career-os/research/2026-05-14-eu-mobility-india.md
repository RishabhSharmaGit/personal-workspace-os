# EU mobility for Indian ICT — Blue Card + per-country paths

Reference for evaluating EU opportunities. Distilled from `eu-legal-gateway.eu` (European Legal Gateway Office in India — official EU initiative for Indian ICT mobility) + general knowledge as of 2026-05.

## TL;DR for a 10-yr Indian SWE

- **EU Blue Card** is the canonical highly-skilled-worker visa across 25 of 27 EU states (Denmark + Ireland opt-out; Ireland has its own Critical Skills Permit — see [[2026-05-14-ireland-relocation]]).
- **Germany, Netherlands, and Spain/Portugal** are the strongest tech hiring markets for Indian engineers in 2026, with explicit Blue Card pathways.
- **Salary thresholds** are per-country and updated annually. For 2026, Germany's regular threshold is ~€48,300 / shortage-occupation (incl. ICT) ~€43,800. Far below US TC but cost of living + tax structure differ.
- **EU Legal Gateway Office** (eu-legal-gateway.eu) is a free, official platform that combines visa guidance + verified employers + Indian-ICT-specific support. Worth registering on if EU is a serious track.

## What is the EU Blue Card?

The Blue Card is the EU-wide work-residence permit for highly-skilled non-EU nationals. Each member state implements its own variant of the directive.

```
1. Job offer from EU-based employer (contract ≥6 months)
       ↓
2. Salary at or above country threshold
       ↓
3. University degree (recognized — your BE from MIT-AOE qualifies)
       ↓
4. Apply at EU consulate in Mumbai/Delhi (or in-country if already there)
       ↓
5. Approval typically 4-12 weeks
       ↓
6. Permit issued for ≤4 years (renewable). Family reunification rights.
       ↓
7. After 33 months in same EU state → EU long-term residence; 21 months if you reach B1 language.
```

## Per-country snapshot (best for Indian SWE in 2026)

### Germany (highest volume)

- **Threshold (2026)**: €48,300 regular / €43,800 ICT-shortage occupation
- **Pros**: Largest EU tech market; Berlin / Munich / Hamburg startup scenes; English-speaking workplaces increasingly common
- **Cons**: 35-45% effective tax + social charges; rental market is brutal in major cities; German bureaucracy (Anmeldung, Steuer-ID) is real
- **Indian-friendliness**: Very high. Germany's Skilled Immigration Act (2024 revision) explicitly targets Indian ICT.
- **Citizenship**: 5 years with B1 German (3 with C1 + integration)

### Netherlands (strongest tech employer mix)

- **Threshold (2026)**: ~€5,688/month for Highly Skilled Migrant (HSM) ≥30 yrs; €4,171/month for <30 yrs. Plus 8% holiday allowance typically.
- **Pros**: 30% ruling (huge tax break for highly-skilled migrants, valid 5 years post-arrival); English ubiquitous; Amsterdam has dense tech ecosystem (Booking, Adyen, Mollie, etc.)
- **Cons**: Housing crisis in Amsterdam/Utrecht; ruling reduced from 30% pre-2024 to phased model
- **Indian-friendliness**: High. Most large tech employers (Booking, Picnic, etc.) sponsor freely.
- **Citizenship**: 5 years, no language requirement for permit-track residents who renounce other citizenship (India doesn't allow dual citizenship — this matters)

### Ireland

See dedicated note: [[2026-05-14-ireland-relocation]]. Ireland opts out of Blue Card; uses its own Critical Skills Employment Permit.

### Spain / Portugal (newer growth markets)

- **Spain Blue Card threshold (2026)**: ~€34,000-€41,500 depending on region
- **Portugal D8 / Tech Visa**: lower thresholds, gentler bureaucracy; Lisbon tech scene grew significantly post-2021
- **Pros**: Lower cost of living; Mediterranean lifestyle filter aligns with your "salary + lifestyle" criterion
- **Cons**: Salaries lower than DE/NL; Spanish/Portuguese language helpful long-term

### Nordics (DK / SE / FI / NO)

- DK has its own Positive List + Fast-track program (not Blue Card). High salary thresholds (~DKK 487k = €65k+).
- Sweden: Blue Card threshold ~€2,300/month minimum but ~€60k+ realistic for skilled tech. Long-term residence in 4 years.
- Finland: Lower threshold (~€3,638/month). Strong gaming/AI scenes.

### France

- **Talent Passport**: 4-year permit, salary >€42,406 (2026). Path to citizenship in 5 years with B1 French.
- Tech scene: Paris (Mistral, Hugging Face HQ, banking), Lyon, Toulouse.
- Tax: high (40-50%) + complex.

### Switzerland / UK

- **Switzerland**: NOT EU; separate work-permit quota system (cantonal). Highest salaries in Europe but quotas tight for Indian nationals.
- **UK**: NOT EU; Skilled Worker visa (formerly Tier 2). See separate notes if you want UK detail.

## EU Legal Gateway Office (India) — the official channel

`https://eu-legal-gateway.eu`

A free EU-funded initiative specifically for Indian ICT professionals. Combines:

1. **Step-by-step visa pathways** per EU country (visas, work permits, skills recognition)
2. **Verified employer leads** — companies committed to recruiting Indian ICT talent
3. **Chatbot + advisory sessions** (free)
4. **Skills-recognition guidance** (your BE from MIT-AOE is generally recognized)

**Action**: register on this platform. It's the highest-signal EU resource you have access to. Track relevant employer leads as `companies/{slug}.md` entries.

## Decision factors for `intake/07-relocation-preferences.md`

- Which country first? (Germany / Netherlands / Spain / Portugal / Nordics / France)
- Language tolerance (will you learn German for a Berlin role? Spanish for Madrid?)
- Salary floor in EUR (varies massively by city — Amsterdam ≠ Lisbon)
- Tax + social charge tolerance (very different across countries — NL's 30% ruling is a HUGE delta)
- Are you OK renouncing Indian citizenship long-term (NL, DE require it for naturalization)?

## Indian-citizenship constraint to remember

**India does not permit dual citizenship.** Every EU country except a few will require renunciation of Indian citizenship to naturalize. Holding Indian citizenship while having EU long-term residence is fine — but EU passport requires giving up Indian one. Many engineers stop at long-term residence (5-year cycle, no naturalization) for this reason.

## Honest assessment for your primary track

EU Blue Card → Long-term residence is a clean 5-year path with stable lifestyle outcomes. Lower total comp than US, but tax-adjusted, fewer surprises. **Best fit if you decide remote-first plateaus and you want a real second-country base.** Germany or Netherlands first; Spain/Portugal if lifestyle weighs heavier than salary.
