# Ireland — Critical Skills Employment Permit (Indian SWE)

Reference for evaluating Ireland relocation. Distilled from `topmate.io/nidhi_nagori` (Ireland Masterclass — ₹1,396 paid event by Nidhi Nagori, May 23 2026, claims 8,300+ sponsor company database) + general knowledge as of 2026-05.

## TL;DR for a 10-yr Indian SWE

- **Ireland opts out of EU Blue Card**; uses its own Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP).
- **CSEP threshold (2026)**: €38,000 generic / **€32,000 for listed-occupation roles** (ICT/software occupations are on the list).
- **Strong English-speaking environment**; major tech employers (Google, Meta, Microsoft, LinkedIn, Stripe EU HQ, Intercom, Workday, etc.) cluster in Dublin and Cork.
- **Pathway to EU citizenship**: 5 years residence on CSEP → permanent residence → 6 months later, Irish citizenship → EU passport. This is the **cleanest EU passport route** of any country for Indian SWEs.
- **Salary ceiling lower than US** but cost-adjusted comp is decent in Cork/Galway; Dublin housing is expensive.

## Critical Skills Employment Permit at a glance

```
1. Job offer from Irish employer (2-year contract)
       ↓
2. Salary ≥€32k (listed-occupation) OR ≥€38k (generic)
       ↓
3. Employer applies for CSEP via DETE
       ↓
4. Approval typically 4-12 weeks
       ↓
5. Indian national: apply for entry visa (Stamp 1G) at Irish consulate Mumbai/Delhi
       ↓
6. Travel to Ireland, register with INIS (Stamp 1 issued)
       ↓
7. CSEP valid 2 years → renewable to 5 years total
       ↓
8. After 21 months: apply for Stamp 4 (no labour market test, free to change employer)
       ↓
9. After 5 years residence: apply for citizenship → Irish passport → EU citizenship
```

## Thresholds and process detail

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| CSEP generic min salary | €38,000 |
| CSEP listed-occupation min salary (ICT) | €32,000 |
| Stamp 1G (entry visa for non-EU) | typically 2-6 weeks after CSEP issue, Indian nationals |
| Stamp 4 eligibility | 21 months on CSEP |
| Permanent Residence | 5 years legal residence |
| Citizenship | 5 years residence (4 of last 8 + 1 final continuous) |
| Spouse / partner | Dependent permit; can work after Stamp 1 |
| Children | Education access immediately |

## Tax & comp reality

- Income tax: 20% up to ~€42k, 40% above
- USC (universal social charge): up to 8%
- PRSI: 4%
- Effective marginal rate at senior IC TC: ~52%
- Dublin rent (2026 est.): €2,500-3,500/mo for 2BR (high vs salary)
- Cork / Galway rent: ~30-40% cheaper
- Senior SWE TC at FAANG-Dublin: typically €110-160k gross (significantly lower than US)

## Decision factors for your case

- Salary floor in EUR — €100-130k+ gross for Dublin to make sense after rent
- Are you OK with the Dublin housing market (genuinely difficult)?
- Cork / Galway alternative? (lower comp but liveable)
- Family / spouse plans — Ireland is family-friendly; dependent permits work
- Long-term citizenship plan? (5-year clean path; Indian citizenship would need renouncing for Irish passport)

## Honest assessment for your primary track

Ireland is the **best "endgame EU passport" option** if you eventually want EU mobility. The 5-year clean path is rare in Europe. **But for immediate salary impact, US > Ireland.** Best as a Phase 2 move after building 1-2 years of remote-from-India runway.
